👋 Hi, I’m Yejin Lee

Undergraduate Student in AI | Robotics

I am deeply interested in robotic manipulation and autonomous navigation systems driven by AI. My current focus is on exploring and extending Transformer-based manipulation architectures such as PerAct, while integrating Hierarchical Diffusion Policies (HDP) to enable multi-stage planning and skill decomposition for complex tasks. In parallel, I study lightweight localization methods using Visual-Inertial Odometry (VIO), aiming to enhance autonomy in real-world settings. Ultimately, I am passionate about integrating these approaches into practical applications like smart factories and logistics automation.

🔍 Research Interests

  • 🧠 Robot Manipulation with Transformer-based Architectures
  • 📦 Diffusion Models for Multi-task Robotic Control
  • 📍 AR-based Indoor Navigation using VIO and QR-based Positioning
  • 🎯 Domain Generalization in Object Detection (e.g., Detectron2 + CBAM)
